McLaren In Formula 1

McLaren is one of the oldest active teams in F1, and has been in competition every year since 1966.

It's the second most successful Formula One team, having won 183 races, 12 Drivers' Championships and 8 Constructors' Championships.

The teams most recent race win was achieved by driver Daniel Ricciardo at the Italian Grand Prix in 2021. The team's first win in 9 years.

Monaco 1966

Equipped with a chassis and a dream, the brand-new McLaren team headed to Monte Carlo for the Monaco Grand Prix.

The livery was a concession to Hollywood film director John Frankenheimer, who was busy filming Grand Prix.

Bruce McLaren drove the car and managed to qualify 10th out of 16, but had to retire the car mid race due to an oil leak on the 10th lap.

How It Started

Belgium 1968

Two years after their first race, McLaren managed to win their first race at the Belgium Grand Prix in 1968.

McLaren were running two drivers, Denny Hulme & Bruce Mclaren who qualified 5th & 6th respectively.

Bruce managed to go ahead and win the race while Denny had to retire the car after just 18 laps due to a halfshaft issue.

The First Win

1974

Fittipaldi

The 1974 season was the 28th Formula one season and consisted of 15 races, starting in Argentina and ending in the US.

The championship fight between McLaren and Ferrai ended up lasting until the final race of the season.

Emerson Fittipaldi finished fourth for McLaren to ensure that he was the World Champion, beating Regazzoni for Ferrari by three points.
